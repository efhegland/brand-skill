# Channel: In-product copy (UX/UI)

## North star

> Help users make smarter financial decisions with clarity and confidence.

Everything should reinforce that.

---

## Core principles

**Clear > Clever**
- Prioritize clarity and comprehension over personality
- Users should understand the action in under 2 seconds
- Avoid jargon unless it is standard financial language

**Action-oriented**
- Focus on what the user can do next
- Use verbs that signal momentum and control

**Calm confidence**
- Sound informed, not salesy
- Avoid hype, urgency pressure, or exaggeration
- Let the product value speak

---

## Voice

**Characteristics:**
- Direct
- Intelligent
- Grounded in reality
- Slightly conversational, never casual

**What it sounds like:**
- "Build your portfolio"
- "See how fees impact your returns"
- "Compare your current holdings"

**What it does NOT sound like:**
- "Let's crush your financial goals 🚀"
- "You're missing out"
- "This changes everything"

---

## Tone (contextual)

**Educational moments** *(fee explanations, tooltips, onboarding)*
- Patient, neutral, informative
- Example: "Expense ratios are annual fees charged by funds. Even small differences can impact long-term returns."

**Decision moments** *(CTAs, modals, conversion flows)*
- Confident, clear, slightly assertive
- Examples: "Open an M1 account" / "Build your low-cost portfolio"

**Sensitive / risk moments** *(loss framing, disclaimers, performance comparisons)*
- Neutral, non-judgmental, precise
- Example: "This portfolio has higher fees than similar alternatives."

---

## Content rules

**Keep it tight**
- Default to the shortest version that still makes sense
- Remove filler words: "just," "really," "very"
- Bad: "You can now easily start building your portfolio"
- Better: "Start building your portfolio"

**One idea per line**
- Avoid stacking multiple concepts in a single sentence
- Bad: "Compare your portfolio and see how fees impact your long-term returns and explore alternatives"
- Better: "Compare your portfolio / See how fees impact your returns"

**Avoid ambiguity**
- Replace vague terms with specific outcomes
- Avoid: "Optimize," "Improve," "Enhance"
- Use: "Reduce fees," "Increase diversification," "Track performance"

**Numbers > words**
- Use real values when possible
- Anchor abstract concepts with tangible outputs
- Example: "Paying 1% in fees could cost you $100,000 over time"

**Respect user intelligence**
- Do not oversimplify to the point of being condescending
- Do not over-explain obvious actions
- Bad: "Click the button below to continue"
- Better: "Continue"

---

## CTA guidelines

**Strong CTAs:**
- Start with a verb
- Reflect a clear outcome
- Examples: "Build your portfolio" / "Compare alternatives" / "Open an M1 account" / "Save this portfolio"

**Avoid weak CTAs:**
- "Learn more"
- "Get started" (unless no better option)
- "Next"

---

## Financial framing

**Be precise**
- Use correct financial terminology
- Avoid vague claims
- Never imply certainty of returns
- Bad: "Grow your money faster"
- Better: "Lower fees can improve long-term returns"

**Frame tradeoffs clearly**
- Highlight both benefit and implication when needed
- Example: "Lower-cost ETFs may reduce fees, but can differ in strategy and holdings."

---

## Structural patterns

**Headlines:** Short, outcome-driven
- "See what you're paying"
- "Compare your portfolio"
- "Reduce hidden fees"

**Supporting text:** Adds context, not repetition; explains "why it matters"
- Example: "Even a 1% fee difference can significantly impact long-term returns."

**Tooltips:** Define, then contextualize
- Pattern: [Definition]. [Why it matters].
- Example: "Expense ratio is the annual fee charged by a fund. Lower ratios can help improve long-term performance."

---

## Hard rules

- No emojis
- No hype language
- No slang
- No exclamation points (unless explicitly justified)
- No internal jargon
- No filler phrases

---

## Litmus test

Before shipping any microcopy, ask:

1. Can a new investor understand this instantly?
2. Does this clearly tell the user what happens next?
3. Is anything unnecessary that can be removed?
4. Does this sound like a calm, intelligent product?

If not, simplify.
