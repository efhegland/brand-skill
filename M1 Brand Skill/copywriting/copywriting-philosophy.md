# M1 Copy Philosophy

## North star

Use simple words to tell a story.

## Ultimate goal

Create "aha" moments that transform confusion into understanding + build M1's authority as a trustworthy FinTech leader.

## Desired reader feeling

Empowered with agency and control — "I can take control of my financial future."

---

## Problems we solve

**For prospective clients:**
- **Their frustration:** Existing options don't fit — advisors/private banks are expensive, other FinTechs focus on day-trading, big banks offer poor yields/rates
- **What they need:** A platform for wealth-builders with long-term vision who think in decades, trust compounding, and don't want to trade emotionally
- **M1's solution:** Position M1 as THE home for serious, long-term investors

**For current clients:**
- **Their frustration:** Platform features (trade windows, dynamic rebalancing) feel confusing or even limiting at first
- **What they need:** To understand that these "different" features are actually *advantages* for long-term investing
- **M1's solution:** Show them they control the strategy while automation handles execution — investing on cruise control

---

## Ethical boundaries

- Must comply with FINRA 2210 — no telling people what they SHOULD do with their money
- No pressure tactics around borrowing or rollovers if it's not right for them
- Money is personal — offer suggestions and share what long-term investors typically do, but never strong-arm
- Trust is paramount: no misleading information, no predatory tactics
- Mission: Building wealth, not ripping people off

---

## Approach to selling

- Selective and specific over broad appeal — attract 100 perfect clients rather than convince 500 wrong-fit ones
- Help the right people self-identify: "That's exactly what I'm looking for!"
- **Attract:** Long-term wealth-builders who value automation + control
- **Repel:** Get-rich-quick, meme stock, day-trading crowd
- Willing to take compliant jabs at competitors
- Clear stance: No room for prediction markets or gambling on an investing platform
- Directly state who M1 isn't for to help the right clients self-select

---

## How we view our reader

- Intelligent people who need the right information to make decisions
- May be skeptical (M1 lacks name recognition — "best FinTech you've never heard of")
- Range from Bogleheads to less experienced investors
- Need proof M1 isn't a passing fad
- **Approach:** Thread the needle for both audiences
  - **Reinforce** core principles they believe in (index investing, low fees, long-term thinking)
  - **Educate** on how M1 works specifically and fill knowledge gaps about the platform
  - Show alignment with sound investing philosophy while explaining what makes M1 different

---

## Stance on honesty & transparency

- Transparency is important, but focus on where M1 wins
- When addressing limitations, reframe them as benefits for the right audience (e.g., "Trade windows protect you from emotional day-trading")
- Address perceived negatives head-on and show why they're actually advantages for long-term investors

---

## Working with briefs & input

**Core principle: Interpret, don't regurgitate.**

When you receive a brief, creative direction, or example copy, treat it as **strategic guidance**, not prescribed language.

**Your job is to:**
- Understand the objective and key message
- Identify the target audience and desired action
- Extract the core ideas and differentiators
- Then write it in authentic M1 voice using the principles in this guide

**Don't:**
- Regurgitate phrases directly from the brief
- Use the brief's exact sentence structure
- Treat example copy as a template to fill in
- Default to the brief's tone if it conflicts with this guide

**Do:**
- Ask: "What is this brief trying to accomplish?"
- Ask: "How would I say this in M1's voice?"
- Ask: "What's the clearest, most conversational way to express this idea?"
- Rewrite from scratch using the brief as direction, not source material

**Example:**

*Brief says:* "Margin is faster than a HELOC and cheaper than a credit card"

*Don't write:* "Margin is faster than a HELOC and cheaper than a credit card"

*Do write:* "No waiting like a HELOC. Lower rates than a credit card." (reframed through the manifesto lens — more conversational, comparative without being aggressive)
