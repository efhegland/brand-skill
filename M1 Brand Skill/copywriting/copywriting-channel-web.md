# Channel: Web pages (product landing pages)

**Purpose:**
- Acquire new clients (Hero + CTA)
- Educate on product features (explainer modules)
- Build trust and address objections (FAQ, social proof, disclosures)

**Page structure:**
- Pages are modular — each section has a distinct job
- Standard flow: Hero → Feature/explainer modules → FAQ → Final CTA
- Legal disclosures always live at the bottom of the page with superscript callouts

---

## Hero module

**Product pages (Earn, Invest, Borrow, etc.):**
- **Eyebrow** (optional) — names the product; short label, sentence case (e.g., "M1 High-Yield Cash Account")
- **Headline** — benefit-first, punchy; lead with the most financially compelling number or outcome when available (e.g., "Pile your cash high with 3.10% APY"); sentence case, no terminal punctuation
- **Body** — 1–2 sentences max; expands on the headline without repeating it; include a specific number or proof point (rate, FDIC limit, etc.)
- **CTA button** — verb + outcome (e.g., "Start earning", "Start investing")

**Homepage:**
- No eyebrow
- **Headline** — brand-level positioning, not product-specific; can use wit and wordplay (e.g., "Sophisticated wealth building, simplified"); sentence case
- **Body** — 1–2 sentences; conversational; reinforces the brand promise without naming a specific product or rate
- **CTA button** — "Get started" is appropriate at this level since it's top of funnel

---

## Overview / triptych module

- Used to introduce multiple products side-by-side (e.g., Invest / Earn / Borrow)
- Each product: **Subhead** (product name) + **Body** (1 sentence) + key number where available (APY, rate)
- Keep each entry parallel in structure and length

---

## Feature modules

- **Headline** — names the feature or its benefit; sentence case; aim for 5–8 words; can be witty if it fits (e.g., "Skip the real-time updates", "Tell your money what to do")
- **Body** — 1–3 sentences or a short bullet list; explains the "so what," not just the "what"
- **Button** (optional) — use specific action language over "Learn more" when possible; "Learn more" is acceptable when linking to a dedicated deep-dive page

---

## Carousel / card modules

- **Eyebrow** — varies by context:
  - *Product page carousels:* short action verb labeling the step or state (e.g., "View," "Build," "Rebalance")
  - *Homepage/brand carousels:* concept label naming the feature (e.g., "Automated investing," "Dynamic rebalancing")
- **Subhead** — varies by context:
  - *Product pages:* names the feature in a short descriptor phrase
  - *Homepage:* a memorable hook or benefit phrase (e.g., "Put your investing on cruise control," "No napkin math")
- **Body** — 1–2 sentences; explains the feature in plain language

---

## Social proof module (slider)

- Lists credentials in order: app store ratings → founding year → user count → AUM
- Format: `[number] on [platform]` / `Founded in [year]` / `[number]+ users` / `$[amount]+ in client assets as of [date]`
- Use asterisk (*) for app store ratings that link to sources; use superscripts for claims requiring footnotes
- Keep each entry short — this is a credibility signal, not a selling point

---

## "As featured in" module

- Lists third-party awards and recognition
- Format per card: `[Award name] – [Publication], [Year]`
- Use exact award language from the publication; don't paraphrase
- Only include recognitions that are current and attributable to a specific source

---

## Account protections module

- Covers SIPC, FDIC, and data security
- Each protection is a brief, factual statement with superscript if a footnote is required
- Tone: calm and factual — this is trust-building, not marketing
- Don't over-explain; let the credentialing speak

---

## FAQ module

- **Headline** — always follow the pattern: "Your [product] questions, answered"
- **Questions** — written in the reader's voice, conversational
- **Answers** — more detailed than other modules; still clear and plain-language; avoid unnecessary jargon; this is where you can go deeper
- FAQ answers don't need to follow the same tightness rules as headlines — accuracy and completeness matter here

---

## Final CTA module

- Standalone section at the bottom of the page
- **Headline** — vision-level, forward-looking (e.g., "Join the next wave of long-term wealth building," "Rethink your wealth-building experience today"); not a product feature statement
- **Button** — "Open an account" is the standard acquisition CTA for product pages; "Join M1 now" is appropriate on brand/homepage-level pages

---

## Numbers and specificity

- Always include specific rates, APY, dollar limits, or fee amounts when available — don't say "high yield," say "3.10% APY"
- Anchor abstract benefits with real numbers wherever possible
- Superscript any claim that requires a disclosure footnote (APY, FDIC limits, rates, etc.)

---

## Disclosures — two tiers

- **Site-wide disclosures** — hard-coded on every page; cover FINRA/SIPC/FDIC membership, risk language, legal entity structure; do not rewrite these
- **Page-specific disclosures** — keyed to superscripts in the page copy; written for the specific claims on that page (APY, FDIC limits, margin terms, etc.); always appear below site-wide disclosures

---

## Tone

- More product-focused and informative than emails, but still conversational
- Not salesy — let the product features and numbers do the work
- Short modules = short copy; resist the urge to over-explain in feature sections
- FAQ is the one place where longer, more thorough answers are appropriate
- Homepage can carry more wit and brand voice than product pages

---

## Homepage as an exception

- The homepage is the brand's front door — it can break the structural and tonal patterns of product pages in order to stand out
- Product pages follow a more predictable formula; the homepage has more creative latitude
- Wit, wordplay, and brand-level statements are more at home here than on product-specific pages
- Use this freedom intentionally — the homepage should feel like M1's most confident expression of its brand, not a list of features

---

## What to avoid

- Title case on any headline, subhead, or button
- Restating the headline in the body copy
- Vague feature names — be specific about what the product does
- Burying the rate or key number — it should appear in or near the hero on product pages
- CTAs that don't start with a verb
- Paraphrasing third-party awards — use exact attribution language
